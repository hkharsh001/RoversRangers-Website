What is The Scout Movement?

Scouting is a global, educational youth movement with the stated purpose of supporting young people in their physical, mental, and spiritual development so they may play constructive roles in society. The movement is based on the principles conceived by its founder, Robert Baden-Powell, in 1907.

In India, the national organization is The Bharat Scouts and Guides (BSG). Formed on November 7, 1950, by merging several pioneering organizations, the BSG administers the programmes for both boys and girls. Its mission is to contribute to the education of young people through a value system based on the Scout Promise and Law, helping to build a better world where people are self-fulfilled as individuals and play a constructive role in society.

The Scout & Guide Promise and Law

The Promise and Law are the foundation of Scouting, representing a personal commitment to the movement's values.

The Promise

“On my honour, I promise that I will do my best—
To do my duty to God and my country,
To help other people, and
To obey the Scout/Guide Law.”

The Law

  1. A Scout/Guide is trustworthy.
  2. A Scout/Guide is loyal.
  3. A Scout/Guide is a friend to all and a brother/sister to every other Scout/Guide.
  4. A Scout/Guide is courteous.
  5. A Scout/Guide is a friend to animals and loves nature.
  6. A Scout/Guide is disciplined and helps protect public property.
  7. A Scout/Guide is courageous.
  8. A Scout/Guide is thrifty.
  9. A Scout/Guide is pure in thought, word, and deed.

Core Principles and Motto

The movement is defined by three fundamental principles:

  • Duty to God: Adherence to spiritual principles, loyalty to the religion that expresses them, and acceptance of the duties resulting therefrom.
  • Duty to Others: Loyalty to one's country in harmony with the promotion of local, national, and international peace, understanding, and cooperation. Participation in the development of society with recognition and respect for the dignity of humanity.
  • Duty to Self: Responsibility for the development of oneself.

The Motto

The motto for Scouts and Guides is "Be Prepared". This signifies being in a state of readiness in mind and body to do your duty. For the senior section, Rovers and Rangers, the motto is "Service," reflecting a focus on active contribution to the community.

The Scout Method

The Scout Method is the principal system for achieving the movement's educational goals. It is a system of progressive self-education through eight interconnected elements:

  • The Promise and Law
  • Learning by doing
  • The team system (or Patrol System)
  • A symbolic framework
  • Personal progression
  • Nature
  • Adult support
  • Community involvement

Advancement and National Awards

Scouting provides a structured path for personal growth, recognized through prestigious awards.

  • Rajya Puraskar: The highest award at the state level (Governor's Award), which marks a high level of proficiency in skills and service. It is a prerequisite for the President's Award.
  • Rashtrapati Award: The highest national-level award for Scouting and Guiding in India, presented by the President of India. Achieving this award is a mark of exceptional character, skill, and distinguished service to the community.

Career and Academic Benefits

The discipline, skills, and values learned in Scouting are highly regarded and can provide tangible advantages in higher education and careers.

Indian Railways Recruitment

The Ministry of Railways provides a special quota for the recruitment of candidates against posts in Level 1 and Level 2 (Group 'C'). President's Scouts/Guides/Rovers/Rangers are eligible to apply under this quota, which gives them preference in the selection process. This is a significant benefit for dedicated members who achieve the highest award.

Other Opportunities

Certificates of proficiency and high-level awards like the Rashtrapati Award are given weightage during admissions to certain universities and in recruitment for various central and state government services, recognizing the holder's leadership, discipline, and service-oriented mindset.

Our Global Connection

The Bharat Scouts and Guides is part of a global community through its affiliation with two main world bodies:

World Organization of the Scout Movement (WOSM)

WOSM is the global confederation of 172 National Scout Organizations, with a membership of over 57 million. Its mission is to contribute to the education of young people, through a value system based on the Scout Promise and Law, to help build a better world.

World Association of Girl Guides and Girl Scouts (WAGGGS)

WAGGGS is the largest international voluntary Movement dedicated to girls and young women, representing over 10 million members across 152 countries. Its mission is to enable girls and young women to develop their fullest potential as responsible citizens of the world.

A Brief History of the Movement

The Scouting Movement was founded by Lord Robert Baden-Powell in 1907 with an experimental camp on Brownsea Island, England.

Scouting was unofficially introduced in India in 1909. On November 7, 1950, The Bharat Scouts and Guides was formed through the merger of The Boy Scouts Association in India and the Hindustan Scout Association. The All India Girl Guides Association joined in 1951.
