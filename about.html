What is Rovers & Rangers?

Rovers (boys) and Rangers (girls) are the senior youth section of Bharat Scouts & Guides, serving ages 15–25. They grow through leadership, service, training, and adventure.

Stages in the Journey

  • Aspirant: Initial stage, min. 3 months, preparation, and Pravesh test.
  • Rover / Ranger: After investiture, full membership.
  • Rover-in-Service / Ranger-in-Service: Advanced roles, mentoring, leading service projects.

Purpose & Objectives

The purpose of the movement is to contribute to young people’s development so they reach their full physical, intellectual, social, and spiritual potential as individuals and as responsible citizens of local, national, and global communities.

  • Leadership & character building
  • Service to society & nation building
  • Environmental awareness and sustainability
  • Promoting peace, respect, and unity

Advancement & Awards

The structure under BSG includes stages like Pravesh, Nipun, Rajya Puraskar, and the prestigious Rashtrapati Rover/Ranger Award. Efficient badge work, service hours, and leadership help you advance.

Uniform & Identity

Uniform items often include a steel-grey shirt, navy trousers (or skirts for Rangers as applicable), scarf / woggle, beret or cap, badges, belt, and black shoes/socks. Uniform represents discipline, unity, and pride.

History of Scouting & Guiding

The Scouting Movement was founded by Lord Robert Baden-Powell in 1907, experimenting with youth education at Brownsea Island in the UK. Over time it spread globally.

In India, Scouting began in 1909. After independence, various Scout and Guide groups merged to form the Bharat Scouts & Guides (BSG) in 1950. Today, BSG is the national body recognized by World Scouting and WAGGGS.

The Rover & Ranger Wing is a key segment within BSG, empowering youth to take leadership, serve communities, and carry forward the ethos of unity and service.
